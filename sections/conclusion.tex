\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have demonstrated that cooperative behaviour emerges reliably when agents share
a global reward signal, even in the absence of explicit communication channels.
Group size effects are significant: coordination speed degrades super-linearly
beyond four agents in our benchmark environments.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
Our environments are relatively low-dimensional. Future work should validate
these findings in continuous-state settings and with heterogeneous agent capabilities.

\paragraph{Broader impact.}
Understanding the conditions for emergent cooperation has implications for
multi-robot systems, autonomous vehicle fleets, and distributed resource management.
